Our Global Vision

At TZARCS, we are proud to play a constructive role in the debates shaping auditing, financial reporting, regulation, and corporate governance around the world.

Regulation and policy

Developments in regulations and public policies can have wide ranging implications for companies and their auditors. For companies, it might mean new reporting or governance requirements. For us as auditors, the nature and scope of the audit, the oversight of our work or the role of the auditor in the capital markets could all change.Developments in regulations and public policies can have wide ranging implications for companies and their auditors. For companies, it might mean new reporting or governance requirements. For us as auditors, the nature and scope of the audit, the oversight of our work or the role of the auditor in the capital markets could all change.


We use our experience and knowledge to bring insights to the ongoing debates around the significant regulatory and public policy issues affecting business, society, our clients and our profession.

We know it is important to stay up to date and engaged with these issues. You can find more information about what has been happening, read our latest regulatory briefing and sign up for updates from us below.

These changes are often driven by efforts to improve transparency, accountability, and stakeholder protection, particularly in light of evolving market dynamics and societal expectations. New regulations may require businesses to revise their financial reporting procedures, adjust to stricter environmental, social, and governance (ESG) criteria, or enhance internal controls to mitigate risk. In some cases, these changes may lead to significant operational adjustments or require companies to invest in new technologies or hire additional personnel to meet the compliance demands.

For auditors, regulatory developments often have a direct impact on the nature and scope of their work. New laws and standards can change the way auditors approach their responsibilities, requiring them to evaluate different types of risks or scrutinize new areas of concern. The introduction of stricter accounting standards, such as those around revenue recognition or lease accounting, necessitates that auditors adjust their audit procedures and adopt more sophisticated techniques to ensure compliance.

Additionally, regulations surrounding audit quality and auditor independence may place more pressure on auditors to maintain objectivity and exercise due diligence in their assessments.
